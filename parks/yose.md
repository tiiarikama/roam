# Yosemite National Park

## Overview
Yosemite National Park sits in California's Sierra Nevada mountains and covers
nearly 1,200 square miles of granite cliffs, waterfalls, ancient sequoias, and
high-alpine wilderness. The park is best known for Yosemite Valley — a seven-mile
glacial valley flanked by El Capitan, Half Dome, and Bridalveil Fall — but the
majority of the park is designated wilderness and largely uncrowded.

Yosemite Valley draws the heaviest visitation and requires timed entry permits
from May through October. Tuolumne Meadows (at 8,600 ft elevation) offers a
quieter, alpine alternative accessible via Tioga Road when it's open
(typically late May through November).

## Trails

### Half Dome
- Distance: 14–16 miles round trip
- Elevation gain: 4,800 ft
- Difficulty: Strenuous
- Permit required: Yes — Half Dome permit via spring lottery or daily lottery.
  Apply at recreation.gov. Approximately 300 permits issued per day.
- Best season: Late May through October (cables installed)
- Notes: Start no later than 6am. The cables section is nearly vertical —
  gloves strongly recommended. Avoid in afternoon thunderstorms.

### Mist Trail to Vernal Fall
- Distance: 3 miles round trip (to Vernal Fall), 7 miles (to Nevada Fall)
- Elevation gain: 1,000 ft (Vernal), 2,000 ft (Nevada Fall)
- Difficulty: Moderate
- Permit required: No (day use)
- Best season: Spring for peak waterfall flow; steps are slippery when wet
- Notes: One of the most crowded trails in the park. Start before 8am.
  The granite steps past Vernal Fall are extremely steep.

### Four Mile Trail
- Distance: 9.6 miles round trip
- Elevation gain: 3,200 ft
- Difficulty: Strenuous
- Permit required: No
- Best season: May through November
- Notes: Outstanding views of Yosemite Valley from Glacier Point.
  Can be done one-way with a shuttle from Glacier Point (summer only).

### Panorama Trail
- Distance: 8.5 miles one way (Glacier Point to Valley)
- Elevation gain: 200 ft gain, 3,200 ft descent
- Difficulty: Moderate (one way downhill)
- Permit required: No
- Best season: June through October
- Notes: Requires shuttle or car spot. One of the most scenic trails
  in the park — passes Nevada Fall, Emerald Pool, and Vernal Fall.

### Tuolumne Meadows Trails
- Multiple trails ranging from easy meadow walks to strenuous peak climbs
- Cathedral Lakes: 7 miles round trip, moderate, permit not required
- Lembert Dome: 2.8 miles round trip, moderate, great panoramic views
- Best season: July through September (Tioga Road must be open)

## Permits & Reservations

### Timed Entry Permits (Valley)
Required for driving into Yosemite Valley: late April through October.
Permits cover entry between 5am and 4pm. Book at recreation.gov up to
90 days in advance on a rolling basis. Released at 8am PT.

Alternatives that bypass the permit requirement:
- Arrive before 5am or after 4pm
- Take the YARTS bus from Merced, Fresno, or Mammoth Lakes
- Stay at a park lodging or campground (reservation acts as permit)

### Half Dome Permits
- Spring lottery: Apply in March for permits covering May–September.
  Apply at recreation.gov. Results announced in mid-April.
- Daily lottery: 50 permits released daily 2 days before the hike date.
  Apply at recreation.gov starting at midnight PT.
- Cost: $10 per person (plus $2 lottery fee)

### Wilderness Permits
Required for all overnight backcountry trips.
- 60% released via reservation lottery starting 24 weeks in advance at recreation.gov
- 40% released as walk-up permits at wilderness centers at 11am the day before
- Quota varies by trailhead — popular trailheads (Happy Isles, Yosemite Valley)
  fill quickly

### Camping Reservations
- Yosemite Valley campgrounds (Upper Pines, Lower Pines, North Pines):
  reserve at recreation.gov, opens 4 months in advance on rolling basis
- Tuolumne Meadows Campground: similar reservation system
- First-come-first-served: Camp 4 (walk-up only, popular with climbers)

## Seasons & Access

### Spring (April–June)
Waterfalls at peak flow from snowmelt — Yosemite Falls, Bridalveil Fall, and
Horsetail Fall (February only) are spectacular. Tioga Road typically opens
late May (snow-dependent). Valley roads may flood in high snow years.
Timed entry permits required from late April.

### Summer (July–August)
Peak crowds and valley heat (can exceed 95°F in the valley). Tuolumne Meadows
and high country fully accessible and much cooler (60s–70s°F). Half Dome
cables up. Shuttle service running throughout the valley.

### Fall (September–November)
Best overall conditions. Crowds thin significantly after Labor Day. Temperatures
mild. Foliage color in late October. Tioga Road closes with first major snowfall
(typically November — can be earlier). Timed entry ends in late October.

### Winter (December–March)
Tire chains required and frequently enforced. Glacier Point Road closed.
Tioga Road closed. Many valley facilities open year-round. Badger Pass ski area
open (downhill and cross-country). Valley is uncrowded and stunning with snow.
No timed entry permits required.

## Getting There & Around

### Getting There
- Nearest airports: Fresno Yosemite (FAT, 65 miles south), San Jose (SJC, 170 miles)
- From San Francisco: ~3.5 hours via CA-120 (Tioga Pass, summer only) or via US-140
- From Los Angeles: ~5.5 hours via CA-99 and CA-140

### YARTS Bus
Year-round bus service from Merced (Amtrak connection) and seasonal service
from Fresno and Mammoth Lakes. Riders bypass timed entry permit requirement.
Book at yarts.com. Strongly recommended during summer.

### Getting Around Inside the Park
- Free valley shuttle serves major trailheads and lodges — use it, parking is extremely limited
- El Capitan Shuttle (summer) covers western valley
- Glacier Point Road open to vehicles when snow-free; no shuttle
- Bikes allowed on valley bike paths (rentals available at Yosemite Valley Lodge)

## Practical Tips
- Cell service is extremely limited throughout the park. Download offline maps
  (Gaia GPS, AllTrails) before arriving.
- Bear canisters required for backcountry. Bear boxes provided at campgrounds
  — use them. Bears actively break into cars; do not leave food or scented items.
- No gas stations inside the park. Fill up in El Portal (closest), Groveland,
  or Lee Vining.
- Yosemite Village has a grocery store (the Village Store), post office,
  medical clinic, and ATM.
- Dogs allowed on paved trails and in campgrounds only. Not permitted on
  unpaved trails, in wilderness, or in park buildings.
- Water is drinkable from taps at campgrounds and lodges. Filter or treat
  backcountry water.
